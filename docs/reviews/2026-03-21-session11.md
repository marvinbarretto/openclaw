## The insight: briefing quality is capped by purpose

We've been optimising briefing *output* (calendar accuracy, email picks, surprise quality) for 11 sessions. The real problem isn't output quality — it's that the briefing has no *operational context*. It reads from static data, delivers a monologue, and moves on. There's no shared task state, no awareness of what's been done, no ability to take action.

The briefing should be a *status report from a task manager*, not a *news broadcast from a curator*.

This reframes the entire project. The briefing isn't the product — the vault-as-task-system is. The briefing is just one interface to it.
