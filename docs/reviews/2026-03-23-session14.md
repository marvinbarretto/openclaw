## The problem: heartbeat reasoning dump

After the clean briefing, Jimbo sent **~8 blocks of internal monologue** from heartbeat checks throughout the afternoon and evening (roughly 15:30–20:00). Each block showed the full chain-of-thought reasoning about whether to act — checking calendar, assessing gaps, evaluating email urgency, listing HEARTBEAT tasks — then concluding "no action needed" with `****`.

This is the entire decision process being narrated to the user on Telegram. Pages of text for zero actionable output.

## Marvin's reaction

"I'm happy and excited that it's more active, it's thinking a lot more, but this shouldn't be leaking into the chat this much."

Key: the activity level is right. The thinking is right. The output filter is broken. Kimi K2 is treating "think about whether to act" as output even when the conclusion is silence.

## Root cause

SOUL.md already says "Never show your working" (line 102). HEARTBEAT.md says "Skip silently" and "Silence is free." But these were too subtle — "Silence is free" frames it as cost optimization, not UX discipline. The model isn't distinguishing between "reasoning" and "output."

## Fix applied

Added an "Output discipline" section at the top of HEARTBEAT.md with hard rules:
- Action needed → short message (1-5 lines)
- No action needed → ZERO output, not even a period
- Never narrate decision process
- Specific anti-patterns called out: "Since:", "Assessment:", "Given that:"

Pushed to VPS via `workspace-push.sh`. Takes effect on next heartbeat cycle.
