## What we learned

1. **The pipeline is the healthiest it's ever been.** Both sessions ran, 11-12 gems, 22-24 calendar events, 23-25 insights all with complete fields, dispatch queue populated. The data layer is solid.

2. **Gemma4 can't use any of it.** 5 vault tasks → "I don't have any." 11 gems → "nothing to report." This is the session 7 gap again: excellent data, terrible delivery. But this time it's a model quality issue, not a bind mount.

3. **Prescriptive briefings are the wrong frame.** Marvin doesn't want "here's your plan for the day." He wants "here's what I see in your world." The shift from advisor to reporter changes the skill design fundamentally.

4. **Self-awareness is missing.** Jimbo has API access (JIMBO_API_URL + JIMBO_API_KEY) and multiple helper scripts, but no way to check his own health. He can't answer "what have I done today?" or "did the pipeline run?" — he just guesses.

5. **Free model rotation changes the quality contract.** When the model varies, the skill must be simpler and more data-driven. A prescriptive skill that requires judgment breaks on weaker models. A reporter skill that presents data works regardless.
