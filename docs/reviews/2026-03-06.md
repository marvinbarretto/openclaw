## What we learned

1. **Email quality is the breakout win this session.** Across all three briefings, the email highlights are specific, personal, and useful. Wizz Air "TODAY ONLY" with travel context, James + Razorlight with "you've previously booked Gerry Cinnamon", Travel Wire with specific article callouts. This is working even WITHOUT the worker pipeline — Sonnet is doing good email triage on its own when it has context files.

2. **The false alert → triple briefing is the worst UX bug.** #19 (morning: missing) triggered Jimbo to re-deliver twice. The user got bombarded with increasingly worse content. The alert system is actively making things worse, not better.

3. **Calendar fabrication is persistent.** Salsa appears in all three sessions (March 4, 5, 6). It's either in a subscribed calendar feed or a reliable hallucination. "Thomas Antony arrival £76.16", "Breakfast" at 14:00, and "Evening meal" at 17:30 are pure fabrication. The daily-briefing skill says "Calendar contains ONLY events from briefing-input.json. Do not add, infer, or fabricate any events" but this rule is ignored in self-compose mode.

4. **Geography gap.** Jimbo doesn't know where Marvin lives relative to London venues. "On your doorstep" for a Victoria pub when Marvin is in Watford/South Oxhey shows missing spatial context. This could be a line in PREFERENCES.md or a context file addition.

5. **"Exposed thinking" is the real ask for the day plan.** Marvin doesn't just want a schedule — he wants to see the reasoning. "I put LocalShout here because your energy is highest in the morning and there are no interruptions until 14:00." This is exactly what the Opus analysis layer is designed to provide (#18).

6. **Vault tasks stale for the third session running.** "Reconsider Angular for Spoons" at priority 9 when Spoons isn't the focus. "Get Gmail AI up" may be done. The scorer either hasn't re-run since PRIORITIES.md was updated, or the scoring model doesn't differentiate well enough at the top.
