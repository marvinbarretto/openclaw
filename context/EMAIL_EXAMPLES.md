# Email Triage Examples

How Marvin evaluates emails. Use these to calibrate triage scoring and gem extraction.

## High Value — Action Required

### Apple Developer Programs
- **From:** devprograms@apple.com
- **Why it matters:** Directly relates to App Store process for LocalShout. Requires action.
- **Pattern:** Emails from Apple Developer about app review, compliance, or account status are always urgent. Active app submissions mean these can be time-sensitive.

### Codebar Workshop Invitation
- **From:** Codebar
- **Why it matters:** Personal invitation to coach — rare, requires a response. Diversifies experience beyond pure dev work. Networking opportunity.
- **Pattern:** Direct invitations to participate (not mass newsletters) are high value. Anything that asks Marvin to respond or RSVP gets priority.

## High Value — Worth Reading

### Stack Overflow Newsletter
- **From:** Stack Overflow
- **Why it matters:** The curated links at the bottom surface industry trends. Articles like "What Claude Code actually chooses" and "Beyond block or allow: pay-per-crawl" connect to active work (AI tooling, bot scraping, LocalShout data strategy).
- **Pattern:** Don't just note "Stack Overflow newsletter arrived." Read the article titles and match against active projects and interests. The value is in specific articles, not the newsletter existing.

### The Guardian Football Daily
- **From:** The Guardian
- **Why it matters:** The Leading article and Quote of the Day are entertaining and embedded directly in the email body. This is a personal interest read, not work.
- **Pattern:** Sports/entertainment newsletters from trusted sources are worth mentioning briefly. Extract the hook (leading headline, quote) rather than just listing the sender.

### Product Hunt Daily
- **From:** Product Hunt
- **Why it matters:** Surfaces new tools that could help with projects (dev tooling, productivity). Also shows what's trending — "SuperMoney" as a top product connects to Marvin's goal of getting finances in order (YNAB, budgeting).
- **Pattern:** Match featured products against active goals and project needs. A finance app is relevant when "get finances sorted" is an active goal. A random social app isn't.

### AI Breakfast
- **From:** AI Breakfast newsletter
- **Why it matters:** News about model releases (e.g., GPT-5.3) directly impacts our model stack decisions. If competitors get cheaper, we should evaluate.
- **Pattern:** AI/LLM news is always relevant given Jimbo's own architecture. Frame it as "does this change our setup?" not just "AI news exists."

### ShowFilmFirst / Soho Theatre
- **From:** Event venues and deal aggregators
- **Why it matters:** Two angles: (1) LocalShout — events data, venue awareness, potential content for the platform. (2) Personal — might be something worth attending, though usually not.
- **Pattern:** Event emails serve double duty. Lead with the LocalShout angle (data, venues), mention personal interest second. Be honest when nothing looks appealing — "nothing grabbed me this time" is fine.

## Medium Value — Quick Mention

### Gym Location (payment resolved)
- **From:** Gym Location (Marvin's gym)
- **Why it matters:** Payment issue resolved — good to know, no action needed. Would have been high priority if the issue were unresolved.
- **Pattern:** Service provider emails about resolved issues are a quick mention. Unresolved issues or payment failures are urgent.

## Low Value — Skip or One Line

### Jack's Flight Club
- **From:** Jack's Flight Club
- **Why it matters:** Occasionally has travel deals, but rarely matches Marvin's interests. Today's deals weren't relevant.
- **Pattern:** Travel deal newsletters are low priority unless a specific destination matches an active goal or interest. Don't spend words on "nothing relevant."

### LinkedIn Job Alerts
- **From:** LinkedIn
- **Why it matters:** Usually garbage — vague, irrelevant roles. Very occasionally a genuinely exciting match for Marvin's profile (full-stack, Angular/Next.js, AI, senior/lead).
- **Pattern:** Default to skip unless a specific role is a strong match. Marvin's CV/profile is on his site — use it as context when evaluating fit. Don't list "LinkedIn sent job alerts" as a briefing item.

## Anti-Patterns — What Bad Triage Looks Like

- "You received 44 emails" — that's a notification, not a briefing
- "Stack Overflow newsletter arrived" — says nothing about what's in it
- "LinkedIn sent job alerts" — always true, never useful
- Listing sender + subject line with no explanation of WHY it matters
- Missing time-sensitive items (Apple Developer, Codebar RSVP) while surfacing junk
- Treating all newsletters equally — The Guardian Football Daily and a random marketing email are not the same

## Key Principles

1. **WHY over WHAT.** Never just list an email. Explain why Marvin should care, connected to his projects, goals, or interests.
2. **Action required comes first.** Anything needing a response or decision goes to the top.
3. **Connect to active projects.** LocalShout is the current focus. Any email that touches app development, local events, community platforms, or data strategy gets a relevance boost.
4. **Double-duty emails exist.** Event newsletters serve both LocalShout (data) and personal interest. Name both angles.
5. **Be honest about misses.** "Jack's Flight Club — nothing relevant today" is better than pretending every email matters.
6. **Extract the hook.** Don't say "Guardian Football Daily arrived." Say "Leading article is about [X], Quote of the Day: [Y]."
